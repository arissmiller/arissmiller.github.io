Class 3 Notes: Ch 2.4 Conditional Probability (May 12)

Defintitions

Conditional Probability:

  • The probability of an event A may have to be adjusted if we know that some related event B has already occurred.

Dpendent Events:

  • When the outcome or occurrence affects the outcome or occurrence of the second event in such a way that the probability has changed.

  • Example: Drawing a card from a deck without replacing it then drwing a second card

  • Example: selecting a ball from a bucket, not replacing it, then selecting a second ball

Multiplication rule for dependent events (Conditional Probability)

  • When 2 events, A and B, are dependent, the probability of both occurring is \(P(A \cap B) = P(A) \cdot P(B \mid A)\)

  • \(P(B \mid A)\) is read as Probability of B given A

  • \(P(A \mid B \) is read as Probability of A given B

Conditional Probability:

  • Any Probability that is revised to take into account the known occurrence of other events.

  • Effect of additional information is to revise (possibly to shrink) the sample space \(S \to S’\)

Conditonal Probability Formula:

\(P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}\) also: \(P(B \mid A) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(A)}\)

Probabilites for “at least” questions (see ex.9 and 10)

  • Multiplication rules for complementary events
  • sometimes it is easier tofind compement than to calculate all possibilities

Calculating unconditional and inverse probabilities:

  • Partitioned sample space, (broken up into n mutually exclusive events)
  • Sample space is union of n independent events, since there is no overlap we do not have to subtract intersections \(S = \bigcup \limits_{i=1}^{n} A_{i}\)
  • No intersection between individual events, written as \(A_i \cap A_j = \emptyset\)
  • \(i \neq j\)
  • \(P(Ai \gt 0)\)
  • The probability for an event is the sum of all conditional probabilities, so
  • \(P(B) = \sum^{n}_{i = 1} P(B \mid A_i) \cdot P(A_i)\)

Baye’s Theorem

  • Extension of conditional probability
  • Inverse Probbility: If we know \(P(B \mid A_i)\) for all \(A_i\), then we can find \(P(A_i \mid B)\)

  • \(P(A_j \mid B) = \frac{P(B \mid A_j) \cdot P(A_j)}{\sum^{n}_{i =1} P(B\mid A_i) \cdot P(A_i)}\)

  • The above means the conditional probability of this event, divided by the sum of all probabilities

  • Questions to ask

    • Is the problem asking for an unconditinoal probability (partitioned sample space where \(\sum P(B \mid A_i)\)) or a conditional probability (Baye’s thm)?

      • If unconditional, let B denote the ecent whose probability you are looking for

      • If conditional, let B denote the event that has already happened

    • Once B is identified, go back and assign all the \(A_i\)’s

A card is dawn from a deck of cards. What is the probability that the card is a Club given that it is a King

\(P(C) = \frac{13}{52}\)

\(P(K) = \frac{4}{52}\)

\(P(K \cap C) = \frac{1}{52}\)

\(P(C \mid K) = \frac{P(K \cap C)}{P(K)}\)

\(P(C \mid K) = \frac{\frac{1}{52}}{\frac{4}{52}} = \frac{1}{4}\)

Given 2 events A and B. P(B) alone = 0.1, P(A) alone = 0.2, \(P(A \cup B)^c = 0.6\) What is \(P(A \mid B)\)

\(0.1 = P(B \cap A^c)\)

\(0.2 = P(A \cap B^c)\)

\(0.6 = P(A \cup B)^c\)

\(P(A \cup B) = 1 - P(A \cup B)^c\)

plug in values and we get

\(P(A \cup B) = 0.4\)

\(P(A \cup B) = P(A \cap B^c) + P(B \cap A^c) + P(A \cap B)\)

plug in values and we get

\(0.4 = 0.2 + 0.1 + P(A \cap B)\)

\(P(A \cap B) = 0.1\)

\(P(B) = P(B \cap A^c) + P(A \cap B)\)

plug in values and we get

\(P(B) = 0.1 + 0.1\)

\(P(B) = 0.2\)

\(P(A \mid B) = \frac{(A \cap B)}{P(B)}\)

plug in values and we get

\(P(A \mid B) = \frac{0.1}{0.2} = 0.5\)

A bucket has 5 white chips, 4 black chips, 3 red chips. Four chips are drawn sequentially without replacement. What is the probability of the sequence {W, R, W, B}

\(P(W_{1}) \cdot P(R_{2} \mid W_{1}) \cdot P(W_{3} \mid R_{2} \cap W_{1}) \cdot P(B_{4} \mid W_{3} \cap R_{2} \cap W_{1})\)

\(\frac{5}{12} \cdot \frac{3}{11} \cdot \frac{4}{10} \cdot \frac{4}{9} \approx 0.02\)

Example 10: A single die is rolled 4 times. Find the probability of getting at least one 6

Example 11: Bucket 1 has 2 red chips and 4 white chips. Bucket 2 has 3 red chips and 1 white chip. 1 chip is drawn from bucket 1 and moved to bucket 2. What is the probability that a chip drawn now from bucket 2 will be red?

\(P(R_2) = P(R_1) \cdot P(R_2 \mid R_1) + P(W_1) \cdot P(R_2 \mid W_1)\)

\(P(R_2) = \frac{2}{6} \cdot \frac{4}{5} + \frac{4}{6} \cdot \frac{3}{5} = \frac{20}{30} = \frac{2}{3}\)

Example 12: A deck of cards is shuffled and the top is removed. What is the probability that the second card will be an ace?

\(P(A_2) = \frac{4}{52} \cdot \frac{3}{51} + \frac{48}{52} \cdot \frac{4}{51} \approx 0.0769\)
