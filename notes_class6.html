Definitions

Variance

  • Written as \(\sigma^2\), var(x)

  • The variance of a random variable X, is the spread, or variability of the distribution.

  • Discrete Random variable: \(\sigma^2 = E[(x - \mu)^2] = \sum_{0}^{k}(x_k - \mu)^2 P(x) = E(x^2) - [E(x)]^2\)

  • Continuous Random Variable: \(\sigma^2 = E[(y-\mu)^2] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}(y-\mu)^2 f(y) dy = E(y^2) - [E(y)]^2\)

  • If \(E(X^2)\) or \(E(Y^2)\) is not finite, then the variance is undefined

Properties of mean/variance

  1. E(ax + b) = aE(x) + b, Var(ax+b) = a^2 Var(x)

  2. The mean of the sum of two independent random variables x + y is the sum of thier means.

Moment:

  • A quantitative measure of the shape of our function

    • 0th moment = total proabability
    • 1st moment = Mean
    • 2nd moment = variance
    • 3rd moment = skewness: long tail? in pos or neg direction?
    • 4th moment = flatness (kurtosis) flat looking/peaky?

Example 1: Give na random variable X (discete) and its associated probability mass function, find the mean and variance

\(\mu = E(X) = \sum_{k=0}^{3}X_k P_x(k) = 1.75\)

\(\sigma^2 = E(X-\mu)^2 = \sum_{k=0}^{3}(X_k - \mu)^2P_x(k) = 0.9375 = \sigma^2\)

Example 2: If you toss a 6 sided die, the probability mass function is uniform. What are the mean and variance?

\(\mu = E(x) = \sum_{k=1}^{6}XP_X(k) = \frac{21}{6} \approx 3.5\)

\(\sigma^2 = E(X^2) - [E(x)]^2 = \sum_{k=1}^{6}X^2P_x(k) - (3.5)^2 = 2.916\)

Example 3: From a previous example, give nthe continuous random variable X with pdf \(f(x) = ex^2 0 \lt x \lt 1\) what are the mean and variance?

\(\mu = E(x) = \frac{3}{4}\)

\(\sigma^2 = E(x^2) - [E(x)^2] = \int_0^1(x^2)(3x^2)dx = \frac{3}{5}\)

\(\sigma^2 = \frac{3}{5} - \frac{3}{4}^2 \approx 0.0375\)

Example 4: Given the probability density function for a continuous random variable X: \(f(x) = (p + 1)x^p 0 \leq x \leq 1\). What are the mean and variance?

\(E(x) = \int_0^1 x(p+1)x^p dx = (p+1)\int_0^1 x^p+1 dx = \frac{p+1}{p+2} \)

\(Var(x) = E(x^2) - [E(x)]^2 = \int_0^1 x^2 (p+1)x^p dx - [E(x)]^2 = \frac{p+1}{p+3} - [\frac{p+1}{p+2}]^2\)
