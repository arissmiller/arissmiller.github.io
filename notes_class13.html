Definitions:

Normal Distributions:

The continuous random variable X follows a Normal Distribution if its pdf is:

\(f_x(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac{1}{2}(\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma})^2}\)

mean = \(\mu\) variance = \(\sigma^2\) \(x~N(\mu,\sigma^2)\)

Charateristics:

  1. Bell shaped peak = maxium = \(x=\mu)\), Inflection peaks at \(x = \mu \pm \sigma\)

  2. All normal curves are symmetric about \(\mu\)

  3. Area under normal curve = 1

  4. \(f(x) \approx N(\mu, \sigma^2) \gt 0\) (All probabilities are positive)\)

  5. \(\lim{x\to\infty} f(x) = 0 \lim{x\to -\infty} = 0\)

  6. The are under normal curve that lies within one standard deviation is ~0.68, about 68% of the total area

  7. 2 Standard deviations of mean ~ 0.95 (95%), 3 standard deviations ~ .997 (99.7%)

“Standard Normal”: \(\mu = 0\),\(\sigma = 1\) ~N(0,1)

Z value:

A number of standard deviations that a particular X is away from the mean

\(z = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu}{\sigma}\)

Are under a standard normal curve = probability

  1. to the left of any z value: use table

  2. To the right of any z value: subtract area to the left from 1

  3. Between 2 z values: subtract are to left of left z from right z
